Analysis — What the Benchmark Reveals About MLLM Judges
Four main findings on judge behavior in physical-adherence (PA) and instruction-alignment (IA) evaluation over 875 in-domain items. Detailed panels, confound controls, and reference exhibits are collected in the Appendix and cross-referenced as (App. A/B/C/D).

§ 1Judges are uniformly weak at physical adherence

PA capped at r ≈ 0.53 · complexity-null
PA agreement with humans caps at Pearson ≈ 0.53 and, unlike instruction alignment, does not track task difficulty — a continuous complexity regression is null (r = 0.047, p = 0.25). The judge cannot even resolve which tasks are physically harder to assess. (Fig. App. C.i; confusion patterns in App. A.)

§ 2Instruction alignment improves with complexity

IA up to 0.61 · r = −0.10, p = 0.015
IA agreement is higher (up to 0.61) and rises with instruction complexity (r = −0.10, p = 0.015). The effect survives control for generation quality (partial r = −0.098 vs raw r = −0.099), so it reflects the instruction itself — longer instructions carry explicit, checkable sub-steps — not a quality confound. (Fig. App. C.i.)

§ 3Judge scores regress to the mean, compressing the top of the leaderboard

top-of-leaderboard resolution loss · not a judge-specific defect
With judge–human correlation below 1, scores show central-tendency regression: best clips are under-scored, worst clips are over-scored. Bias(judge − human) decreases monotonically with human gold across all 8 judge × dimension pairs; on PA, flash goes from +0.60 at human = 1 to −1.14 at human = 5; gemini-2.5-pro from +0.07 to −3.21.

We confirm this is regression to the mean, not a judge-specific defect — every judge's per-quality bias lies within ±0.15 of its own regression baseline E[J|H] = μJ + r · (σJ/σH)(H − μH). The benchmark-relevant consequence: the quality range is compressed, strong generators (human 4 vs 5) are squeezed together, and the judge loses ranking resolution exactly at the top — reflected in the normalized Kendall distance (K_norm) inflating among the strongest models on the leaderboard.

§ 4Judge accuracy is invariant to embodiment

generation quality varies · judge error flat
Generation quality differs sharply by embodiment (human-hand highest, gold PA 3.38 vs ~2.95), yet the judge's absolute error is flat (MAE ≈ 0.93 – 1.05) both raw and after reweighting each embodiment to a common quality mix. Per-embodiment correlation gaps are thus a confound of the true-score distribution, not judge competence — the judge favors no embodiment. (Fig. App. C.ii.)
Baselines. Mode-baseline exact PA = 0.281 / IA = 0.278; human IAA (n = 10) PA Pearson 0.336 / IA 0.354 — the noise floor every judge is measured against.

BBootstrap 95% confidence intervals · per-judge Pearson

which judge gaps are significant
Bootstrap resampling of 875 items × 1000 replicates per judge. CIs among the top PA judges (flash / seed2.1 / seed2.0) overlap, so the PA leaderboard has no unique winner. IA shows a real separation. Also justifies the <15% -coverage cutoff for the leaderboard (CIs widen to ±0.15 below that threshold).
