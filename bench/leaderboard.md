## Baselines

Reference metrics — none are task-grounded judges; all shown to demonstrate that cheap automatic metrics can't measure physical correctness. **Distance metrics are converted to a [0,1] "higher = more real/consistent" score before correlating** (Pearson is scale-invariant, so this only flips sign to positive; magnitude unchanged). Correlations are vs **human PA** (physical adherence).

**Per-item no-reference baselines** (one score per video, no GT needed; fold alongside judges):

| Baseline | what | cov | PA-Pearson [95% CI] | PA-Spearman | IA-Pearson |
|---|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|
| vjepa-similarity | V-JEPA feature-space closeness to real-video manifold (per-item decomp of FVD-JEDi) | 88.7% | **+0.140** [0.071, 0.209] | +0.142 | +0.121 |
| frame-consistency | 1 − raw adjacent-frame pixel diff | 100% | **+0.123** [0.059, 0.185] | +0.118 | −0.008 |
| warp-consistency | 1 − optical-flow warp residual (temporal consistency) | 100% | **+0.105** [0.041, 0.169] | +0.099 | −0.023 |
| random-uniform | random 1–5 floor | 100% | **0.000** [−0.063, 0.065] | 0.000 | 0.000 |

- Strongest (vjepa +0.140) > frame +0.123 > warp +0.105 > random 0 — but **all weak (|r|≈0.1, weakest-judge tier)**: even learned-feature-space distance can't measure physics per-item → task-grounded judges needed. Pearson ≈ Spearman → relationships are monotonic (no non-linearity artifact).
- IA: the raw ≈0 correlations for frame/warp are a **suppression artifact, not independence**. Controlling for PA, both turn significantly **negative** (partial r = −0.105 / −0.110, 95% CI excludes 0), and a distribution-free check agrees — the effect has the **same sign in all 5 gold-PA strata** and is strongest at PA=1 (frame −0.62 / warp −0.65), weakening as gold PA rises: cleanly monotonic for warp (−0.65 → −0.08), decreasing but flattening in the top strata for frame (−0.62 → −0.13, with PA=4→5 essentially level, −0.138 → −0.126). This gradient is not a ceiling artifact — within-stratum gold-IA SD is flat across strata (0.99–1.17, PA=5 no narrower than PA=1), and the gradient survives normalising each difference by that SD. Mechanism: both scores are `1 − minmax(dist)`, so a **motionless** video (robot stalled / never executed) minimises both inter-frame difference and flow residual and therefore scores near-**maximal** — a static clip is the global optimum of these two metrics *by definition*. They reward non-execution, most damagingly among already-physically-failed samples. vjepa's raw IA (+0.121, CI excludes 0) is significant but **fully PA-mediated**: partial r drops to +0.045 with CI containing 0, and its stratified signs alternate (+0.61/−0.13/+0.07/+0.34/−0.36), i.e. no IA signal independent of PA. Only random-uniform is genuinely 0. (Partial correlation: Alice; stratified check: Yu; both B=4000 paired bootstrap, gold PA↔IA = +0.603. Caveat: partial correlation assumes linearity/normality and is not a formal causal-mediation test; vjepa covers a smaller subset of the set than the other two, i.e. a different denominator.)
- ⚠️ **vjepa coverage 88.7%**: 99 videos undecodable (decord), and drops skew toward **weak** generators (abot/cosmos3/cogvideo ~18% vs strong gens ~0%); since the score anti-tracks bad videos, +0.140 is a slight **lower bound** on |corr| (full coverage ≈ slightly higher).

- **large-N-7** drops the 3 small-N generators (kling/veo31/happyhorse, N 25–37) whose small samples inflate FVD and flip the all-10 correlation toward 0/negative.
- ⚠️ Even de-confounded, **τ_ap stays ≈0.1**: FVD orders the garbage tail right (cogvideo bottom) but **mis-ranks the best generators** (human top = wan26/veo31/happyhorse; FVD "most-real" top = abot/cosmos3/gigaworld). So the flattering large-N Spearman (+0.607) reflects tail-ordering, not the judge-relevant top discrimination.
- Conclusion: FVD/JEDi = weak model-level proxy, double-confounded (sample-size + top-mis-ranking); "looks like real video" ≠ "physically correct".
